Debre Tabor Polytechnic College History

Brief History

The College(DTPTC) had been initially established in 1989 E.C by Amhara National Regional State, South Gondar Zone of the Region, at Debretabor Town, 98 KMs far from the capital city of Amhara region, Bahir Dar, 7 KMs far from the Historical and the first Technological place of Africa known as “Gafat” to provide technical & vocational training in 10+1 and 10+2 programs by the name called “Debretabor Skill Development Center” (DSDC)” with having three Departments as Electric city, Building construction and General mechanics. Then, after functioning for four years in September 1994 E.C, DSDC had been changed and renamed its name as, “Debretabor Technical and Vocational Institute.” The occupations during those times were, Electric city, Electronics, Surveying, Drafting, Secretarial science, General mechanics, Auto mechanics, Machining, Wood work, ICT, Accounting and Health Extension.

Later, In September 2000 E.C, the former Debretabor Technical and Vocational Institute were totally upgraded and renamed as Debretabor Technical and Vocational College. Starting from September 2000 E.C to February 2003 E.C, the college had been providing level one, level two, level three and level four programs in 8 sectors: Construction, Textile, Hotel & Tourism, Manufacturing, ICT, Electro-Technology, Automotive, Accounting and Business in one campus. The year from February 01/2003 E.C onwards, the status of the college upgrades and has been renamed as, “Debretabor polytechnic college.” The college has given training in many sectors starting from level one up to level five. Since the establishment of the College, 1989, the college has trained a lot of trainees/graduates, and has played a significant role in the effectiveness of thousands of rural and urban agriculture and health extension workers in the agricultural and health sector; and produced middle level skilled workers in the manufacturing and service industries which have paramount help in the country's efforts to transition to an industrial economy. The statistical data regarding the departments, programs, number of enrolled, graduated, competent, and employed students and number of certified trainers are provided in detail as below. Now a days, the college provides a comprehensive range of full time, extension and short term trainings in 22 occupations from level I up to V. Currently the college is selected as one of the beneficiaries TVETs under Education for Skills and Employability (EASE -Ethiopia) project which is financed by World Bank, to make the college’s Center of Excellence in Animal production and Health, Garment Textile and Automotive Technology (AHGTA) for the next five years. AHGTA has the primary objective to provide competence based training and education in the broad field of Animal production and Health, Garment Textile and Automotive Technology. AHGTA has envisioned becoming the leading flagship TVET institute in Africa in offering internationally recognized training, Maintenance, acting as a think tank for industry solution in Animal production and Health, Garment Textile and Automotive Technology by 2035 G.C. To provide competency based training resulting in innovative, well skilled workforce and solution in the field of Animal production and Health, Garment Textile and Automotive Technology the college working at National and Regional level for public and industry partners. The college believes that creating strong linkage and cooperation with DTU is the corner stone for enhancing the Quality of the training program, technology transfer, research and community services in order to meet the demand of the industry.
